# 🧩 Data Architecture & Reporting Systems

> Organizations have data everywhere, but actionable decisions nowhere.

Business decisions rarely fail because of insufficient data. More often, they fail because information is fragmented, KPIs are inconsistent, reporting lacks context, and operational data is not organized for decision-making.

This catalog organizes reusable decision-support systems around recurring challenges in data architecture, reporting design, KPI standardization, and business information management. Rather than building enterprise data platforms, these systems help transform operational data into reliable, decision-ready information.

---

## Are you experiencing any of these situations?

As organizations grow, data accumulates across spreadsheets, operational systems, and reporting tools. The challenge is no longer collecting information—it's making information trustworthy and useful.

If any of the situations below sound familiar, you're likely facing a data architecture and reporting decision challenge.

- Reports are rebuilt manually every reporting cycle.
- Different departments produce different answers using the same underlying data.
- KPI definitions change depending on who prepared the report.
- Dashboards display metrics, but managers still don't know what action to take.
- Operational data exists across multiple systems without a consistent reporting structure.
- Significant time is spent preparing reports instead of interpreting business performance.

---

## Decision Areas

This catalog covers five recurring areas of data architecture and reporting decision-making.

| Decision Area | Typical Decisions |
|---|---|
| **Data Integration** | Consolidate operational data from multiple systems into a consistent analytical foundation. |
| **Reporting Architecture** | Design reporting structures that answer recurring business questions instead of generating isolated reports. |
| **KPI Standardization** | Define consistent business metrics that remain stable across departments and reporting periods. |
| **Business Performance Analysis** | Transform operational data into meaningful business insights that support management decisions. |
| **Decision Information Systems** | Build lightweight decision-support environments that improve visibility without enterprise software complexity. |

---

## Typical Business Questions

Decision-makers often ask questions such as:

- How do we transform operational data into reliable business decisions?
- Which data sources should be trusted when reports disagree?
- What business questions should our reporting actually answer?
- Which KPI definitions need to be standardized across the organization?
- How can we build repeatable reporting without implementing enterprise BI platforms?
- Which operational signals deserve management attention?
- How can reporting become a decision-making process rather than a reporting exercise?

## Related Decision Domains

Reliable information architecture supports every business decision domain.

- 💰 **Profitability & Financial Decisions** – Financial reporting depends on trusted data models, standardized KPIs, and consistent business metrics.
- 📈 **Marketing & Growth Decisions** – Marketing attribution, campaign evaluation, and customer analytics require integrated reporting frameworks.
- 📦 **Inventory & Supply Chain Decisions** – Inventory planning relies on accurate operational data, inventory visibility, and trusted reporting.
- ⚙️ **Operations & Resource Decisions** – Operational management requires timely reporting, standardized KPIs, and decision-ready performance information.
