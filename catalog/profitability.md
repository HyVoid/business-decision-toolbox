# 💰 Profitability & Financial Decisions

> Revenue is growing, but profit and cash flow are not.

Financial decisions are rarely limited by a lack of data. More often, they are limited by the ability to connect operational activities with their financial consequences.

This catalog organizes reusable decision-support systems around recurring financial decisions involving profitability, cash flow, pricing, investment, and capital allocation. Rather than focusing on spreadsheet features or financial models, it focuses on helping decision-makers answer the questions that matter most.

---

## Are you experiencing any of these situations?

Many financial problems don't appear suddenly—they develop gradually as businesses grow more complex.

If any of the situations below sound familiar, you're likely facing a profitability and financial decision challenge.

- Sales continue to grow, but net profit remains flat or declines.
- Cash is consistently tight, even when revenue and receivables appear healthy.
- Pricing decisions are made without a clear understanding of contribution margin or profitability.
- Products, customers, projects, or business units appear profitable until shared costs are considered.
- Investment opportunities are evaluated without a structured way to compare scenarios and assumptions.
- Different reports produce different financial answers, making it difficult to trust the numbers.

---

## Decision Areas

This catalog covers five recurring areas of financial decision-making.

| Decision Area | Typical Decisions |
|---|---|
| **Profitability Analysis** | Understand where profit is created or lost across products, customers, projects, or business units. |
| **Cash Flow Management** | Forecast liquidity, improve cash visibility, and evaluate the financial impact of operational decisions. |
| **Pricing & Revenue Decisions** | Evaluate pricing strategies, revenue opportunities, and commercial trade-offs. |
| **Investment & Capital Allocation** | Compare financing options, acquisition opportunities, and investment scenarios before committing capital. |
| **Financial Visibility & Performance** | Build consistent financial reporting that supports operational and strategic decision-making. |

---

## Typical Business Questions

Decision-makers often ask questions such as:

- Are we actually profitable?
- Why is cash always tight despite healthy revenue?
- Which products, customers, or projects generate the highest margin—and which destroy it?
- Should we adjust pricing, reduce costs, or change our product mix?
- Can we afford this investment under different business scenarios?
- Which assumptions have the greatest impact on financial outcomes?
- How much downside can the business absorb before financial risk becomes unacceptable?

## Related Decision Domains

Financial decisions rarely exist in isolation. They often overlap with other operational decision areas.

- 📦 **Inventory & Supply Chain Decisions** – Inventory levels, replenishment strategies, and working capital directly affect cash flow and profitability.
- 📈 **Marketing & Growth Decisions** – Pricing, customer acquisition, and marketing ROI ultimately determine sustainable profit growth.
- ⚙️ **Operations & Resource Decisions** – Operational efficiency, project execution, and resource allocation influence both costs and margins.
- 🧩 **Data Architecture & Reporting Systems** – Reliable financial decisions depend on consistent reporting, trusted KPIs, and decision-ready data.
