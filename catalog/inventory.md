# 📦 Inventory & Supply Chain Decisions

> Inventory keeps growing while operational efficiency declines.

Inventory problems rarely begin in the warehouse. They usually originate from disconnected purchasing decisions, inaccurate demand assumptions, poor inventory visibility, and a lack of coordinated supply chain planning.

This catalog organizes reusable decision-support systems around recurring inventory and supply chain decisions involving replenishment, inventory optimization, warehouse operations, purchasing, and working capital management. Rather than focusing on inventory transactions, it focuses on helping decision-makers balance product availability, operational efficiency, and financial performance.

---

## Are you experiencing any of these situations?

Inventory challenges often develop gradually as product portfolios expand, sales channels multiply, and supply chains become more complex.

If any of the situations below sound familiar, you're likely facing an inventory and supply chain decision challenge.

- Inventory continues to increase while available cash becomes increasingly constrained.
- Some products remain in stock for months, while others repeatedly run out.
- Purchasing decisions rely on experience rather than consistent replenishment logic.
- Different warehouses or business units report conflicting inventory positions.
- Demand forecasts, purchasing plans, and actual inventory levels are disconnected.
- Managers spend more time reconciling inventory than improving inventory performance.

---

## Decision Areas

This catalog covers five recurring areas of inventory and supply chain decision-making.

| Decision Area | Typical Decisions |
|---|---|
| **Inventory Visibility** | Build a trusted view of inventory across locations, channels, and business units. |
| **Inventory Planning & Replenishment** | Determine what to purchase, when to reorder, and how much inventory should be held. |
| **SKU & Assortment Optimization** | Identify products that create inventory risk, excess stock, or unnecessary complexity. |
| **Warehouse & Supply Chain Operations** | Improve inventory movement, warehouse coordination, fulfillment efficiency, and logistics planning. |
| **Working Capital Optimization** | Balance inventory investment with customer service levels, operational efficiency, and cash flow. |

---

## Typical Business Questions

Decision-makers often ask questions such as:

- Which products are tying up the most working capital?
- What should we replenish, and when should we reorder?
- Which SKUs should be discontinued, consolidated, or reduced?
- How much inventory is actually healthy for the business?
- Where are the operational bottlenecks across the supply chain?
- Why do different warehouses report different inventory positions?
- How can we reduce inventory without increasing stockout risk?

## Related Decision Domains

Inventory decisions are closely connected with other business decision areas.

- 💰 **Profitability & Financial Decisions** – Inventory directly influences working capital, cash flow, profitability, and return on invested capital.
- ⚙️ **Operations & Resource Decisions** – Warehouse operations, fulfillment processes, and resource planning determine inventory execution efficiency.
- 📈 **Marketing & Growth Decisions** – Marketing campaigns and sales forecasts shape demand patterns that drive inventory decisions.
- 🧩 **Data Architecture & Reporting Systems** – Reliable inventory decisions require integrated operational data, consistent KPIs, and trustworthy inventory reporting.

---

> **Inventory performance is improved by better replenishment and planning decisions—not by holding more inventory.**

> The purpose of this catalog is to help decision-makers improve inventory visibility, optimize working capital, coordinate supply chain operations, and select the decision-support systems that best support effective inventory decisions.
