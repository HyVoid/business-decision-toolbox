# 📈 Marketing & Growth Decisions

> Marketing spend increases, but nobody knows what actually generates profit.

Marketing teams rarely suffer from a lack of data. Instead, they struggle to connect marketing activities with measurable business outcomes and make confident investment decisions.

This catalog organizes reusable decision-support systems around recurring marketing decisions involving budget allocation, attribution, campaign performance, customer acquisition, and sustainable business growth. Rather than focusing on marketing reports or advertising platforms, it focuses on helping decision-makers understand what is working, what is not, and where to invest next.

---

## Are you experiencing any of these situations?

Marketing becomes increasingly difficult to manage as channels multiply, customer journeys become more fragmented, and reporting grows more complex.

If any of the situations below sound familiar, you're likely facing a marketing and growth decision challenge.

- Campaign reports show plenty of activity, but little evidence of business impact.
- Marketing channels are evaluated using clicks, impressions, or leads instead of profitability.
- Budget allocation is based on historical habits rather than measurable return.
- CRM, advertising, and sales data cannot be connected into a consistent decision-making process.
- Agency reports describe performance but provide little guidance on what should change next.
- Customer acquisition continues to grow while retention, profitability, or lifetime value remain unclear.

---

## Decision Areas

This catalog covers five recurring areas of marketing decision-making.

| Decision Area | Typical Decisions |
|---|---|
| **Marketing Performance** | Measure marketing effectiveness using business outcomes instead of activity metrics. |
| **Budget Allocation** | Allocate marketing investment across channels, campaigns, and customer segments based on expected return. |
| **Customer Acquisition & Growth** | Understand acquisition performance, customer value, and sustainable growth opportunities. |
| **Marketing Attribution** | Connect CRM, advertising, and sales data to identify the activities that truly drive business performance. |
| **Marketing Governance** | Improve reporting quality, agency accountability, and decision-making through consistent performance measurement. |

---

## Typical Business Questions

Decision-makers often ask questions such as:

- Which marketing activities actually generate profitable growth?
- Which channels deserve additional investment—and which should be reduced?
- Which campaigns create valuable customers instead of simply generating traffic?
- Where is the customer acquisition funnel losing the greatest value?
- Which marketing reports can actually be trusted when making budget decisions?
- How should next month's marketing budget be allocated?
- Which customer segments create the highest long-term business value?

## Related Decision Domains

Marketing decisions are closely connected with other business decision areas.

- 💰 **Profitability & Financial Decisions** – Marketing investment should ultimately improve profitability, cash flow, and return on investment.
- ⚙️ **Operations & Resource Decisions** – Growth initiatives require operational capacity, execution discipline, and resource planning.
- 📦 **Inventory & Supply Chain Decisions** – Marketing demand directly influences inventory planning, replenishment, and fulfillment decisions.
- 🧩 **Data Architecture & Reporting Systems** – Reliable marketing decisions depend on trusted attribution models, integrated reporting, and consistent business metrics.
